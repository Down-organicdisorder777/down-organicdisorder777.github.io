## 🚀 Getting Started

Once the installation ends, find the cloudsip.app icon on your desktop. Double-click the icon to launch the application. The program opens a window ready for your credentials. 

To make calls, you need account details from your phone service provider. You will need:

*   **SIP Server Address:** The link to your phone provider.
*   **Username:** Your extension or phone number.
*   **Password:** The secure code provided by your administrator.
*   **Port:** The connection port for signals.

Enter these details in the settings tab. Click save to store your configuration. The status indicator shows green when the connection succeeds. You are now ready to make calls.

## 🛠️ Using Features

The interface displays your current calls and phone controls. Use the dial pad to enter numbers manually. Click your contacts list to dial saved people. 

### Managing Calls
When a call arrives, a notification appears on the screen. Click the green button to answer. Click the red button to hang up. During a call, buttons appear for common tasks. 

*   **Hold:** Pauses the audio stream until you press resume.
*   **Transfer:** Sends the call to another person or extension. Select the destination number and press the transfer button.
*   **Mute:** Turns off your microphone. 
*   **Recording:** Captures audio from the call. Files save automatically to your computer storage.

### Using Multiple Lines
The software handles several calls at once. Switch between lines by clicking the tabs at the top of the window. When a new call comes in while you are busy, the software alerts you. You can place the first caller on hold to answer the second caller. 

## 📁 Managing Records

The app keeps track of your history. Every call shows up in the logs. Click the history tab to see:

*   Date and time of calls.
*   Duration of conversations.
*   Incoming or outgoing status.
*   Recordings of past calls.

You can delete these logs at any time via the settings menu.

## 🔧 Troubleshooting Tips

Most connection problems stem from incorrect settings. If the status light stays red, check these items:

1. **Internet Check:** Ensure your computer has an active web connection.
2. **Settings Check:** Re-enter your username and password. Look for typos.
3. **Hardware Check:** Verify your headset connects to the correct port. Use the sound settings in Windows to ensure the microphone works.
4. **Firewall:** Sometimes your security software stops the connection. Ensure the program has permission to access the network.

## 🌐 Technical Background

This application relies on WebRTC and SIP. These technologies turn your browser-based engine into a telephony tool. It supports common industry standards like PJSIP and Asterisk. This ensures compatibility with most office phone systems. 

The software runs locally on your machine. All your data stays within the application interface. We do not store your call information on external servers. This protects your privacy and security.
